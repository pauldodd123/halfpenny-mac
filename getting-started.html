Getting Started

From waitlist to working Mac mini. Usually under 24 hours start to finish.

i

Join the waitlist

Head to the homepage and enter your email address. Pick the tier that fits — Budget (M1), Starter (M2), Pro (M4), or Max (M4 Pro). If you're not sure, the FAQ has a breakdown of each.

We onboard in small batches, so you'll get a reply with your confirmed slot and a price sheet. Once you've confirmed and paid, provisioning starts.

Tip: If you need Xcode, a specific macOS version, or a particular tool pre-installed, mention it in the reply to your slot confirmation — we'll set it up before handing over.

ii

Provisioning

Once payment is confirmed we provision your machine. This means a fresh macOS install, our baseline hardening profile applied via MDM, Tailscale installed and configured, and your SSH public key added.

You'll get an email with:

  • Your Tailscale invite link
  • Your machine's Tailscale hostname (e.g. node-05.your-tailnet.ts.net)
  • Your username on the machine
  • Instructions for adding your SSH public key if you haven't already

Provisioning typically completes within a few hours; the 24-hour window covers edge cases and time zones.

iii

Set up Tailscale

Tailscale is how you reach your Mac. It creates an encrypted peer-to-peer connection — no public IP, no port forwarding, works from anywhere.

Install Tailscale on your local machine:

  • Mac: tailscale.com/download/mac or brew install --cask tailscale
  • Linux: curl -fsSL https://tailscale.com/install.sh | sh
  • Windows / iOS / Android: available from tailscale.com/download

Join the tailnet: Click the invite link from your provisioning email. You'll be prompted to sign in with your Tailscale account (or create one — free tier is fine). Once joined, your Mac mini will appear in your Tailscale device list.

Verify it's working: Once both devices are on the tailnet, you should be able to ping your Mac mini by its Tailscale hostname. Give it a minute for the peer-to-peer handshake to complete on first connection.

iv

SSH access

SSH access is key-based only — no passwords. You'll need to provide your SSH public key when you sign up (or shortly after). If you haven't set one up before:

# Generate a key if you don't have one ssh-keygen -t ed25519 -C "your@email.com" # Print your public key to copy/paste cat ~/.ssh/id_ed25519.pub

Send us the output of that last command (your public key — the one ending in your email). We add it to your account and confirm when it's ready.

To connect:

ssh yourusername@your-node.your-tailnet.ts.net

Your exact hostname and username are in the provisioning email. Once connected you're in a standard macOS shell — zsh by default.

Tip: Add an entry to ~/.ssh/config so you can just run ssh halfpenny:

Host halfpenny HostName your-node.your-tailnet.ts.net User yourusername IdentityFile ~/.ssh/id_ed25519
v

Remote desktop

For a full graphical desktop, you have two options — both work over Tailscale:

macOS Screen Sharing (built-in): Open Finder, press ⌘K, and enter:

vnc://your-node.your-tailnet.ts.net

Sign in with your account credentials. Screen Sharing works well for occasional use.

Jump Desktop (recommended for regular use): Install Jump Desktop on your local machine. Use Jump Desktop Connect on your Mac mini (we pre-install it on request) for a smoother, lower-latency connection. Jump Desktop handles reconnections gracefully and performs better over variable connections.

Note: Remote desktop connections go over your Tailscale tunnel — they're never exposed to the public internet. No extra firewall rules needed.

vi

Installing software

You have a standard user account. Most software installs without needing admin rights:

  • Homebrew — works out of the box for CLI tools
  • App Store apps — install from your own Apple ID
  • Regular .dmg / .pkg installers — most work at user level

For anything that requires an admin password — system extensions, kernel extensions, some developer tools, VPN clients — just email us with what you need. We'll install it and confirm. No extra charge, no faff.

Pro tier customers have root access and can install admin-level software themselves.

Homebrew quick-start: If it's not already installed, paste this into your SSH session:

/bin/bash -c "$(curl -fsSL https://raw.githubusercontent.com/Homebrew/install/HEAD/install.sh)"
vii

Backups and snapshots

We run nightly snapshots of your home directory to Backblaze B2, encrypted at rest. Snapshots are kept for 14 rolling days.

To request a restore, email us with:

  • The date you want to restore from (e.g. "yesterday's snapshot")
  • Whether you want a full home directory restore or specific files/folders

We aim to complete restores within 4 hours during UK business hours.

A few things snapshots don't cover: system-level files, apps installed outside your home directory, and anything you've explicitly stored outside ~. For critical data, keep your own off-device backups too.

viii

Getting help

Email is the main support channel — reply to any message from us, or use the address on your invoice. You'll get a reply from Paul directly, not a support bot.

Max tier customers have a dedicated Slack channel for quicker back-and-forth.

Common things we can help with:

  • Installing admin-level software
  • Snapshot restores
  • Xcode or toolchain setup
  • CI/CD runner configuration
  • Tailscale connectivity issues
  • Upgrading or downgrading your tier
  • Any questions not covered in the FAQ

If something is down or broken, email with "URGENT" in the subject and we'll prioritise it.
